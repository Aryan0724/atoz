Stage 2 — qualify & filter
First, remove the leads you should NEVER message. Then classify the rest into 4 types. This stage takes 2–3 mins per lead.
Step 1 — disqualify immediately skip these
If ANY of these are true, remove the lead from your list right now.
Large chain gym — Gold's Gym, Anytime Fitness, Cult.fit, etc.
Last Instagram post older than 1 month — they're inactive
Already has excellent website with booking form, good speed, 150+ reviews
No contact channel found anywhere — can't reach them
Profile is private and no other contact info exists
Step 2 — classify the lead into 1 of 4 types
Tick all checks for each type. Whichever type matches = that's their classification.
Type 1 — no website + high competition
No website link anywhere in bio or Google listing
3 or more competitor gyms exist within 3 km of them
Search "gym in [their locality]" on Maps and count
At least one competitor ranks above them or they don't appear at all
Type 2 — bad website + running ads
They have a website but it loads slowly or looks broken on mobile
Running Facebook or Instagram ads (check Meta Ad Library)
No clear booking button or lead form on their website
Type 3 — no website + low/no competition
No website link anywhere
Fewer than 2 competitors in their area
Located in a smaller town, colony, or outer area
Type 4 — active Instagram + no website (personal trainers)
500+ followers, posts regularly (within 2 weeks)
No website or proper booking link in bio
People asking about price or bookings in their comments

Stage 4 — research
Two tracks. Your team follows only the track matching the list the lead was assigned to.
Zone 1 — identity 10 mins total for all 5 zones
Find owner name from Instagram bio, Google reviews, or Facebook page
Owner signs their name in review replies — check "owner response" section
Find WhatsApp number from JustDial or Google listing
Find one specific thing to compliment — a post, achievement, or content piece
Must be genuine. Fake compliments are obvious and kill the message immediately.

Zone 2 — digital audit
Open their website on mobile — start a timer, note exact load time in seconds
Under 3s = fine. 3–6s = problem. 6s+ = serious — most users have already left
Is there a visible booking/call/enquiry button without scrolling?
Is there a lead capture form (name + phone + submit)?
Search their gym name on Google — do they appear on page 1?
Write down exactly 2–3 specific problems found. Word for word into the message.
Specific = credible. "No booking button" beats "bad website" every time.

Stage 5 — write the message
Every message has 4 parts. Never skip a part. Fill the fields below and your message builds automatically.
The 4-part message structure
Part 1 — hook
One specific thing you noticed about THEM. Shows you looked. Not generic.
Part 2 — pain + competitor
What problem you found. What the competitor is doing instead. The money they're losing.
Part 3 — proof
One result you got for another client. Short. No long story. Just the number.
Part 4 — soft ask
A 10-min call. Not "buy from us." Low pressure. Easy to say yes to.
